Adam Frees: about me.

I've been hooked on physics ever since high school, where I was introduced to topics like quantum mechanics, black holes, and entropy. These were things that I had heard about in science fiction books and movies, but had never considered topics that scientists really studied. At Brown University, I was given the opportunity to explore many different fields during my freshman year, eventually leading me to be convinced that I was most interested in studying physics. Before receiving my bachelor's degree from Brown, I worked for a summer as a researcher at what would become my graduate institution, University of Wisconsin - Madison. I've been a graduate student at UW since the Fall of 2013, working for Mark Friesen and Susan Coppersmith. In my free time, I play racquetball and watch bad movies.
